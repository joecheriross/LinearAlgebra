3. Prove: If \( V,W \in \Reals^n\) are unit vectors, then \(\vert V.W \vert \leq 1 \)

4. Let \(V, W \in \Reals^n \) be vectors that are orthogonal to each other. Prove that \( \|V+W\|^2 =\|V\|^2 + \|W\|^2 \)
Answer: \(\|V+W\|^2= (V+W).(V+W)\)
\(= (V.V) + 2(V.W) + (W.W)\)
\(= \|V\|^2 + \|W\|^2 \) Since V and W are orthogonal to each other \(V.W =0\)

11. Prove or Disprove: If \(W_1\) and \(W_2\) are two subspaces of a vector space V, then \(W_1 \cap W_2\) is also a subspace of V .
Answer: This is true. To prove show \(W_1 \cap W_2\) is closed under addition and scalar multiplication. Consider 2 vectors f and g in \(W_1 \cap W_2\). We have to show \(f + g \in W_1 \cap W_2\) AND \(\lambda f \in W_1 \cap W_2\). Since \(W_1 \cap W_2 \subset W_1\), and \(W_1\) is a subspace, \(f + g \in W_1\) and \(\lambda f \in W_1\). Similarly, since \(W_1 \cap W_2 \subset W_2\), and \(W_2\) is a subspace, we know that \(f + g \in W_2\) and \(\lambda f \in W_2\). So \(f + g \in W_1 \subset W_2$ and also $\lambda f \in W_1 \cap W_2\). This shows that \(W_1 \cap W_2\) is closed under addition and scalar multiplication, so it is a subspace of V .
